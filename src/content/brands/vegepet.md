## About

VegePet is manufactured by Benefit Pet Supplies International Ltd., headquartered in Syuejia, Tainan, Taiwan. The brand produces vegetarian dry dog food formulated by animal nutrition experts using production technology sourced from the EU and US. Their recipes are designed to meet the same nutritional benchmarks as conventional meat-based diets.

For APAC plant-based pet owners, VegePet is notable as one of the **few Taiwan-origin vegetarian dog food brands** with verifiable retail distribution across Singapore and Malaysia — meaning local store access, no international forwarding required.

## Product range

- **Holistic Vegetarian Dry Dog Food** — complete formula for adult dogs, available in 2 kg and 8 kg bags
- **Cheese Flavour Adult Dog Food** — same base formula with plant-based "Nature Vegan Flavour" for palatability
- **Grain-Free Vegetarian Formula** — potato/tapioca starch base for dogs with grain sensitivity (hydrolyzed protein reduces allergen load)

Core ingredients: hydrolyzed vegetarian protein (soy, maize), rice/potato/tapioca starch, pearl barley, oat, alfalfa, flaxseed (Omega-3 source), vegetable oil (Omega-6 source), brewer's yeast, sesame, dehydrated seaweed, yam, burdock, apple, cranberry, plus vitamins and minerals.

## Strengths

- **Taiwan-origin brand** — made in the same APAC country as VIRIDA, with regional supply chain awareness
- AAFCO-complete — meets formal "complete diet" standards, not a supplement or mixer
- Accessible price-point ($$) relative to imported US/EU vegan brands
- Grain-free variant available, useful for dogs with grain sensitivities alongside meat allergies
- Stocked in mainstream Singapore pet chains (Pet Lovers Centre), not just specialty stores
- Ingredient list includes functional botanicals: burdock, seaweed, cranberry

## Caveats

- **"Vegetarian" branding, not "vegan"** — the brand's own marketing uses the term *vegetarian*, and no external vegan certification (PETA, Vegan Society, etc.) appears on their packaging or website. Ingredient lists reviewed show no animal-derived ingredients, but buyers who require certified-vegan labelling should contact the manufacturer to confirm before purchasing.
- **Contains soy and corn** — dogs with soy or corn allergies cannot use the standard formula; the grain-free line uses tapioca/potato starch as base but still contains hydrolyzed soy protein.
- **Limited transparency** — the English-language website is minimal; founding year, ISO/HACCP certification status, and third-party lab testing records are not publicly documented.
- **No wet food** — dry kibble only; dogs that require wet food supplementation for health reasons will need a separate product.
- **Distribution limited to SG and MY within APAC** — not stocked in Hong Kong, Japan, or Thailand at time of writing.
